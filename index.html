Why potatoes matter

Potatoes fuel over a billion people every day. They are naturally gluten-free, pack more potassium than a banana, and adapt to almost every cuisine on Earth. Sustainable growers are reimagining how we plant and plate this humble root.

A quick history lesson

Track potatoes from Andean terraces to global food security hero.

  1. 8000 BCE

    Indigenous communities in Peru domesticate wild potatoes on terraced mountain farms.

  2. 1570

    Spanish explorers introduce potatoes to Europe, where botanists first cultivate them as curiosities.

  3. 1845

    The Irish potato famine reveals the dangers of monoculture and inspires agricultural reform.

  4. Today

    Modern breeders develop climate-smart potatoes that thrive in drought and salty soils.
